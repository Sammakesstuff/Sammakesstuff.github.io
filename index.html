01 // About

Who I Am

Born and raised on the Sunshine Coast, I've spent most of my life reverse-engineering electronics and figuring out how systems tick. That curiosity is what eventually pulled me into amateur radio — getting my Foundation licence in March 2025 was honestly one of the better decisions I've made.

Before getting on air I was deep in the SWL (shortwave listening) world via Broadcastify, soaking up everything I could. These days I'm working toward my Advanced ticket, regularly attending SCARC's Thursday night meetings, and visiting Wednesday sessions during the holidays.

I'm also in Air Cadets, which means regular radiocomms duties — though that's mostly CB channels, which is a bit of a vibe shift from running 75W into my Diamond CP6S vertical and making the neighbour's blender turn on.

03 // On Air

Preferred Modes & Activities

FT8

Primary digital mode. Weak signal DX on the FT-991A — watching the grid squares pile up.

SSB / Sideband

A good ragchew is hard to beat. Nothing like a proper HF conversation.

POTA

Parks on the Air activations. Love getting out portable and making contacts from the field.

Portable Ops

Occasional portable setups away from the guest bedroom shack. Fresh air included.

SWL

Where it all started — years of shortwave listening on Broadcastify before getting licensed.

Cadets Comms

Radiocomms duty in Air Cadets. Mostly CB channels — a long way from FT8 DX.
